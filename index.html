About me

Hi! I am a research intern at the Department of Computer Science and Technology, University of Cambridge, with an MEng in Computer Science from Trinity College, University of Cambridge. I have a strong enthusiasm in deep learning, particularly in self-supervised learning and graph representation learning. I am also very interested in reinforcement learning and generative models. My current research interest is to explore machine learning methods that involve no or less manual data labelling or augmentations, in order to reduce the reliance of specialised human knowledge on the tasks. In my Master’s dissertation, I developed a conceptually novel, principled, task-agnostic, and stable framework for evaluating GNN expressivity through contrastive self-supervision, supervised by Prof Pietro Liò, Dr Dominique Beaini and Hannes Stärk. I am currently working on designing new attention mechanisms for GNNs to operate on hypergraph data, supervised by Dr Aaron Zhao and Prof Robert Mullins.

Outside of academics, I am a keen clarinettist, a 2-kyu beginner kendoka, and a goalkeeper. I am a member and soloist of the Cambridge University Chinese Orchestra Society, and performed in the orchestra’s Michaelmas Recitals and Annual Concerts in 2018, 2019, 2020 and 2022. I am also a member of the Cambridge University Kendo Society, and won the 2nd Place as a university kendo team player in the 2020 University Taikai.
